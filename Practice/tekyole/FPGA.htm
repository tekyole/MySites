- What is FPGA?
Field Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGA) are chips that are consisted of programmable logic blocks and interconnects. They can be loaded by a programming pattern to create a specific digital function. There are a lot of similarities between FPGA and ASIC design processes and therefore often FPGA designers use many of the same tools that ASIC designers may use.
-----

- Explain the general structure of an FPGA?
Configurable Logic Blocks (CLB) formed by (Look Up Tables (LUT) and Flip Flops or Latches) are the programmable elements inside a FPGA. The LUTs and flip flops are organized in slices which mean that those elements shares connections.

- What is difference between an FPGA and a CPLD?
FPGA:
(a) SRAM based technology.
(b) Volatile: Must be reprogrammed once the power is off.
(c) Usually used for complex logic circuits.
(d) Much more expensive.

CPLD:
(a) Flash or EPROM based technology.
(b) Non-Volatile: Need not be reprogrammed once the power is off.
(c) Usually used for simpler or moderately complex logic circuits.
(d) Very cheaper than FPGA.
