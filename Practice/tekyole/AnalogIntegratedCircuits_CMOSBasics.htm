- In NMOS why is the substrate connected to Ground and in PMOS to VDD?
The idea is to reverse bias the drain-substrate and source-substrate junctions. Because the reverse bias junctions almost carry no current and this will prevent losing current to the substrate.
-----

- Explain the fundamental difference between a MOSFET and BJT
The major difference is in how they are controlled. BJT is a current controlled device and MOSFET is a voltage controlled device.
-----

- Why a BJT transistor has higher gain than an MOS transistor?
Gain in a MOSFET transistor can be described by its transconductance, that is the ratio of its drain current to its gate-source voltage in saturation region of operation. The drain current in this region is proportional to the square of (gate-source voltage minus threshold voltage), while in a BJT transistor (in the active operation region) the collector current is expectationally proportional to the base emitter voltage. Since an exponential relationship increases much faster than a square relationship, a BJT has higher gain than a MOSFET transistor.
